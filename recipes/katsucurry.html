Katsu Curry Recipe

Chicken Katsu Curry, a japanese dish that consists of lightly battered chicken, rice and curry sauce. The perfect comfort dish to warm you up on cold nights or bad days. It's also really easy to make!

Ingredients:

  • Curry Sauce
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 2 medium onions, chopped
    • 3 and 1/2 cups water, or as needed
    • 1 container curry sauce mix, S&B brand
  • Chicken Katsu
    • 5 ounces skinless, boneless chicken breast
    • salt and black ground pepper to taste
    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 large beaten egg
    • 1 cup panko bread crumbs
    • 1/2 cup high temperature frying oil

Recipe Steps:

  1. Step 1

    2. Heat oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Add onions to the hot oil and fry until soft and starting to caramelize, about 8 minutes.

    Add carrots. Pour in water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until carrots are soft, about 30 minutes.

  2. Step 2

    3. Remove from the heat and add curry sauce mix; stir well until all pieces are dissolved.

    If you'd like to add other seasonings, do so now. Simmer for 10 to 15 minutes, or longer if you'd like it thicker.

  3. Step 3

    4. While the curry is infusing, flatten the chicken breasts with a kitchen mallet to a thickness of 1/4 inch.

    Season with salt and pepper. Drench in flour, then egg, and then coat generously with panko bread crumbs.

  4. Step 4

    5. Heat oil in a separate saucepan to 355 degrees F (180 degrees C).

    Carefully lower breaded cutlets into the hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 3 minutes per side.

  5. Step 5

    6. After the chicken has cooked, slice them into strips and plate your curry and rice. You're done!
